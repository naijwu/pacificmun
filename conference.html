Pinnacle HarbourFront Hotel

Located in the heart of downtown Vancouver, PacificMUN 2018 will be hosted at the Pinnacle Hotel Harbourfront, an essential piece in PacificMUN’s goal of creating a memorable conference for delegates. Inside the hotel, delegates will be able to enjoy a variety of service amenities, stylish guest rooms, and comfortable yet professional meeting spaces.


In addition to the beautiful rooms and gracious services that the Pinnacle offers, the hotel also boasts an exceptionally convenient location. The Pinnacle is just a walking distance away from many popular attractions, including Robson St, Gastown, and Stanley Park. Furthermore, the Pinnacle is also near many transit options, including the bus, SeaBus, and SkyTrain, thus making it a very accessible venue.

Keynote Speaker

Adam Goldenberg


Adam Goldenberg is an award-winning Canadian lawyer, writer, and non-profit leader. A trial and appellate litigator at McCarthy Tétrault LLP and an Adjunct Professor of Law at the University of Toronto, Adam grew up and attended high school in Vancouver, where he co-founded Western Canada’s first student-run Model United Nations conference. He went on to earn degrees from Harvard and Yale, work as chief speechwriter to the Leader of the Opposition in the Parliament of Canada, serve as Law Clerk to the Chief Justice of Canada, and contribute to the Globe and Mail, National Post, Maclean’s, and CBC News: The National.

Adam has been recognized as a “Leader To Be Proud Of” by Start Proud and is a recipient of the Meritorious Service Medal for his role in co-founding Teach For Canada, a not-for-profit organization that works with First Nations communities to recruit, prepare, and support outstanding teachers. Adam also chairs the Board of Directors of Action Canada, Canada’s premier public policy leadership organization.

At Pacific Model United Nations, the Secretariat team is dedicated to providing all delegates and teachers with a secure, respectful, and rewarding experience. To ensure a safe and professional environment, delegates are required to comply with the following:


Delegates must attend all committee sessions unless excused by their respective head delegate or sponsor teacher. An excuse of absence must also be communicated to the delegate’s respective dias via email.


Delegates must wear conference-issued name tags for identification and security reasons.


Delegates with medical conditions that may impact their participation during the conference must notify their delegation’s primary contact (head delegate or sponsor teacher).


Delegates are prohibited from using substances or objects that are deemed illegal by the Government of Canada, Province of British Columbia, or City of Vancouver.


Delegates must remain in their rooms between the hours of 11:59PM and 7AM unless involved in a committee session that is organized by PacificMUN.
